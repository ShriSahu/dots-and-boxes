Missing Features

    No onboarding/tutorial at all; most casual players will bounce if their first game is confusing or they lose badly with no explanation. Add a 3–4 step interactive tutorial overlay on the first match (and a “?” button to replay it).

    Zero meta‑progression beyond coins and a flat leaderboard; there’s nothing that says “come back tomorrow” beyond the +5 coin login. You need a simple level/xp system, player profile level, and small milestones (e.g., “Win 3 games on 4×4 to unlock X”).

    No structured challenges / missions; add daily and weekly quests like “Win 2 online matches”, “Capture 10 boxes in one game”, “Win without undo”. Tie them to coin or cosmetic rewards and show a simple progress bar.

    Missing proper matchmaking / rating in online mode; a bare room‑code lobby is fine for friends but useless for solo players who want fair opponents. At minimum, add a simple ELO or hidden MMR and “Quick Match” that auto‑pairs players.

    No analytics / A/B hooks at all; if you want to iterate like a serious product, you need basic events (game start/end, mode, grid size, quit point, retention events) and flags to test things like reward amounts or timers.

AI‑specific missing pieces:

    No adaptive difficulty; your Easy/Medium/Hard are static. Add a system that tracks player performance (win rate, blunders like giving away long chains) and quietly nudges AI behavior up or down.

    No AI coach / hint mode; this is a perfect game to show “good vs bad” moves. Add an optional “Hint” button that highlights 1–2 recommended moves and briefly explains why (“This avoids giving a chain of 3 boxes”).

    No AI‑generated challenges/daily puzzles; you could use an AI routine to generate specific board states where the player has to find the winning line sequence. This gives you “Daily Challenge” content without hand‑crafting.

    No personalization; all players see the same options and flows. Use simple bandit logic or AI scoring to suggest “Best mode for you” on the home screen (“You seem to like 4×4 vs AI Hard, try 5×5 Ranked next”).

    No social hooks besides a planned share button; you’re missing friends lists, lightweight emotes, or simple “recent opponents” for rematching.

Top 5 missing features for retention + shareability:

    Short, interactive tutorial + “first session” guided flow.

    Daily/weekly quests + simple progression (level/xp, titles, or badges).

    Proper online quick‑match with basic rating/matchmaking.

    AI adaptive difficulty + hint/coach mode for solo play.

    Daily AI‑generated challenge puzzle + one‑tap share of result.
